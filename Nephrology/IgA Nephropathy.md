Epidemiology:
- Most common primary glomerulonephritis
- Male predominance, teen-30s, higher prevalence in East Asian and Caucasians

Pathophysiology:
- Mucosal immune infection (URI, GI infection, etc.) -> Galactose-deficient IgA production -> Auto antibody formation -> Immune complex deposition in mesangium -> Glomerular damage, inflammation, and scarring
- "Synpharyngitic hematuria" = Hematuria at same time of infection

Presentation:
- Episodic gross hematuria during or after URI classically
- Asymptomatic urinary abnormalities
	- Microscopic hematuria
	- Mild proteinuria
	- Often found incidentally on UA
- Progressive kidney disease
	- Persistent proteinuria = strongest predictor of progression (>1g/day)
	- HTN
	- Declining GFR
	- Progression to ESRD

Workup:
- UA shows dysmorphic RBCs, RBC casts, proteinuria
- BMP to evaluate kidney function
- Serologies
	- Complement usually normal
	- Can exclude lupus, PSGN, etc. 
- Kidney biopsy gold standard

DDx:
- PSGN -> Weeks after infection
- Lupus nephritis -> Positive autoimmune serologies
- Alport syndrome -> Hearing loss, FH
- Thin basement membrane disease -> Isolated hematuria with benign course

Treatment:
- ACEi/ARB
- BP control
- Immunosuppression if refractory or higher risk patient
