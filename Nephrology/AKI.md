Definition:
- Increase in serum creatinine >0.3 over 48 hours
- Increase in baseline serum creatinine >1.5x over 1 week
- UOP <0.5 cc/kg/hr over 6 hours

Assessment:
- Seizure, trauma, or found down? -> CK for rhabdomyolysis
- AMS, anorexia, nausea, metallic taste? -> Uremia
- Rash? -> AIN
- Check recent NSAID and ACEi/ARB use

Plan:
- UA, urine lytes, urine urea, urine creatinine -> FeNa and FeUrea
- Obstruction or stone concern? -> Renal US or CT AP
	- Look for hydronephrosis
- Proteinuria? -> C3/C4, ANCA, anti-GBM, ANA, anti-dsDNA, HBV/HCV/HIV, SPEP, serum free light chain
- Biopsy last resort

Treatment:
- Assess volume -> IV fluids vs diuretics
- Dialysis -> AEIOU
	- Acidosis pH <7 w/ bicarb
	- Electrolytes refractory hyperkalemia
	- Intoxication with ethylene glycol or methanol
	- Overload with anuria
	- Uremia causing encephalopathy, clinically significant pericarditis
- AIN? -> 
	- Stop offending med
	- Start prednisone 40-60 mg QD for 1-2 weeks
- Post-renal? -> 
	- Place foley
	- Alpha-antagonists (tamsulosin)
	- BPH? -> 5-alpha-reductase inhibitors (finasteride)
	- Malignant obstruction? -> Percutaneous nephrostomy
