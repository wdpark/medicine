**Biochemical markers of Kidney Function**
- Serum creatinine is proportional to muscle mass (young, men, Black)
	- Low muscle mass patient -> low serum creatinine -> perceived as better GFR -> overestimation of GFR

**Estimation of GFR**
- Creatine clearance (CrCl) is a better measure of GFR than serum creatinine, but is affected by accuracy of collection and tubular creatinine secretion

**Interpretation of the UA**
- Specific gravity (1.005-1.030) reflects hydration status and ability to concentrate urine
- Blood (+) but RBC (-)? -> consider rhabdomyolysis or intravascular hemolysis
- Leukocyte esterase and nitrites have high PPV and NPV for UTI
- Dysmorphic RBC -> evaluate for glomerulonephritis
- WBC (+) but culture (-)? -> sterile pyuria
	- Consider vaginitis/cervicitis, prostatitis, AIN, kidney stones, or Chlaymydia/Mycobacterium/Ureaplasma UTI
	- No WBC does **NOT** rule out AIN

- RBC casts -> glomerulonephritis
- WBC casts -> AIN
- Granular muddy brown casts -> ATN

- Calcium oxalate
	- Hypercalciuria, hyperoxaluria, calcium oxalate stones, ethylene glycol poisoning
- Calcium phosphate
	- Distal RTA, TLS, acute phosphate nephropathy
- Uric acid
	- Hyperuricemia, gout, DM, obesity, TLS
- Struvite
	- Alkaline urine 2/2 urease-producing UTI
- Cystine
	- Cystinuria

**Measurement of Albumin and Protein Excretion**
- Proteinuria/albuminuria quantification? -> urine protein-Cr ratio
- High urine albumin -> glomerular injury
	- Absence can rule out most glomerular diseases
- No need to monitor urine protein if on ACEi or ARB already
