Diagnosis and Initial Evaluation
- Kidney biopsy or clear anti-PLA2R positive case with nephrotic syndrome and preserved kidney function
- Biopsy should show sub-epithelial immune deposits
- Serologic testing:
	- Anti-PLA2R abs
	- Anti-THSD7A if ^^ negative
	- Secondary causes:
		- Autoimmune (ANA, complements)
		- Infections (HBC, HCV, HIV)
		- Malignancy
		- Drugs (NSAIDS, etc.)
- Differentiate primary vs. secondary MN

Risk stratification:
- Low risk:
	- Proteinuria <3.5 g/day
	- Stable kidney function
	- Normal albumin
	- Often spontaneous remission
- Moderate risk:
	- Persistent nephrotic syndrome
	- Stable eGFR
- High risk:
	- Persistent proteinuria >8 g/day
	- Declining eGFR

Supportive Therapy:
- ACEi/ARB
- BP control <130/80 usually
- Sodium restriction
- Diuretics for edema
- Statins if indicated
- Anticoagulation if albumin very low (<2-2.5 g/day)

Indications for Immunosuppressive Therapy:
- Persistent nephrotic syndromed despite supportive care
- Declining eGFR
- High risk of progression

Immunosuppressive Options:
1) Rituximab
	- Anti-CD20 monoclonal ab
2) Cyclophosphamide + Glucocorticoids
	- Modified Ponticelli Regimen
	- Alternating monthly steroids and oral cyclophosphamide
3) Calcineurin Inhibitors (Cyclosporine or tacrolimus)
