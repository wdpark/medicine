Assessment:
- Na <120 or AMS? -> ICU and q1-2 hr lab draws -> 3% NS 100 ml bolus to increase Na 4-6
- Assess diet and alcohol use -> Beer potomania or tea/toast -> Not enough solute intake to produce urine -> Hypotonic fluid retention
- Assess PMH of CHF, cirrhosis, ESRD, malignancy
- Assess volume status
- Look at glucose to see if Na correction calculation is needed
- DDx:
	- Hypovolemia + low intravascular volume (3rd spacing) -> ADH release -> lowers serum osmolality
	- Infection, malignancy, meds, brain lesion -> SIADH
	- ESRD

Plan:
- Serum osm <300? -> Hypotonic
	- Else -> Pseudohyponatremia from hyperglycemia or protein
- Urine osm >100? -> ADH active
	- Else -> Primary psychogenic polydipsia, tea/toast, beer potomania
- Urine Na <30? -> RAAS active -> Hypovolemia or 3rd spacing 2/2 CHF or cirrhosis
	- Else -> Salt wasting via SIADH, diuretic use, ESRD, or endocrine etiology
- Still unclear? -> TSH, lipid screen, SPEP/UPEP, serum cortisol and ACTH, UDS, BNP

Treatment:
- Goal is to correct 4-6 over 24 hours
- Severe with symptoms? -> 3% NS 100 ml bolus over 10 min -> Raise Na 1-3 -> Up to 3x to get Na up 4-6
- Severe without symptoms? -> 3% NS IV fluid until Na >125
