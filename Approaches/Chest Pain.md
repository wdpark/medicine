**Pericardial Effusion**
- Malignant
- Inflammatory
	- Autoimmune
	- Rheumatic fever
- Infection
	- TB
- Cardiac
	- Pericarditis
	- Post-MI
	- Iatrogenic
	- Trauma
	- Type A aortic dissection
- Metabolic
	- Uremia
	- Hypothyroidism
- Medications
	- Drug-induced SLE
	- Chemo/immunotherapy
	- Hypersensitivity
- Idiopathic
	- Most common, presumed viral


---
- Look for pulsus paradoxus
	- BP drops during inspiration bc increased preload increase expands RV, causing LV collapse leading to diminished BP
	- Check with manual

- Look for cardiomegaly on CXR
- Look for pericardial effusion on ultrasound
	- Volume not as important as rate of accumulation

- Think about acute pericarditis criteria

Treatment:
- High dose NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, aspirin) for 1-2 weeks until chest pain resolved and CRP normalized, then taper
- Colchicine for 3 months until resolved, then taper
