Assessment:
- Need to characterize main phenotype:
	- Malar rash, photosensitivity, discoid rash
	- Raynauds (triphasic color change in cold weather)
	- Livedo reticularis
	- Dry eyes, dry mouth (severity should be pathologic)
	- Miscarriage history
	- Oral ulcers
	- Non-erosive arthritis
	- Renal (hematuria, proteinuria)
	- Serositis (pleuritis, pericarditis)
	- Blood (hemolytic anemia, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia)
	- Neurologic (seizures, psychosis)

- Medications (hydralazine, procainamide, isoniazid)

Subtype classifications:
- Acute cutaneous (heals without scarring or atrophy):
	- Malar rash (spares nasolabial folds in contrast with rosacea)
	- Subepidermal blisters
	- Lupus hair
	- Oral/nasal ulcers
	- Periorbital edema

- Subacute cutaneous (Anti-Ro/SSA and Anti-La/SSB co-positive usually):
	- Annular/polycyclic rash (ringworm-like), similar to tinea corporis
	- Papulosquamous (psoriasiform), resembles psoriasis/eczema
	- Chest, upper back, shoulders, arms, usually spares face and knuckles (unlike dermatomyositis)
	- Post-inflammatory pigmentation
	- Can be drug-induced
	- Anti-Ro crosses placenta -> neonatal lupus (transient rash, congenital heart block)
	- KOH to rule out tinea if annular morphology

- Chronic cutaneous (scarring):
	- Discoid lupus
		- Carpet-tack sign on scale removal -> shows keratin spikes plugging follicles
		- If on scalp -> Permanent scarring alopecia, hypopigmented scar
	- Lupus panniculitis (nodules in subq fat)
	- Chillblain lupus
		- Violaceous plaques on fingers/toes/ears triggered by cold
		- Anti-Ro (+)
	- Lichenoid lupus/lichen planus
		- Violaceous flat-topped papules
	- Tumid lupus
		- Urticarial-like, no surface change, very photosensitive


Plan:
- ANA (sensitive, not specific)
- Anti-dsDNA (specific, tracks activity)
- Anti-Smith (highly specific)
- Anti-Ro/La (neonatal lupus, secondary Sjogrens)
- Antiphospholipid panel (aCL, B2-GP1, lupus anticoagulant)
- C3/C4 (low = active disease)
- CBC, CMP, UA with micro, urine protein:Cr

- Proteinuria >500 mg/day or active urine sediment? -> renal biopsy

Treatment:
- All patients:
	- Hydroxychloroquine 200-400 mg/day
		- Annual eye exam (retinal toxicity ^^)
	- Sunscreen SPF 50+

- Flares/Moderate-Severe:
	- Prednisone
	- Azathioprine or mycophenolate
	- Belimumab for refractory non-renal (Benlysta)
