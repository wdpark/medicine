**Most Common**
- Intra-axial mass = most likely metastasis
- Primary intra-axial mass
	- Adults: Glioblastoma multiforme
	- Children: Medulloblastoma (PNET)
- Extra-axial mass
	- Meningioma

**Brain Metastasis**
- Most common intra-axial mass
- Primary Malignancies
	- Lung, Breast, Melanoma
- Signs
	- Well-defined
	- Near gray-white junction
	- Multiple or solitary
	- Surrounding edema
	- Enhances with contrast

**Glioblastoma Multiforme** (Gliomas)
- Most common primary intra-axial mass in adults
- Poor prognosis
- Male > Female
- 65-75 yo
- Signs
	- Supra-tentorial
	- Necrotic and infiltrative
	- Cross midline (butterfly glioma)
	- Enhances with contrast
	- Significant vasogenic edema and mass effect

**Medulloblastoma**
- Most common primary intra-axial tumor in children
- Primitive neuroectodermal tumor (PNET)
- Male > Female
- Peak age 3-5 years
- Signs
	- Infratentorial to cerebellum
	- Effacement of 4th ventricle and basal cisterns
		- Hydrocephalus
	- Hyperdense
	- Necrosis
	- +/- calcifications
	- Enhances with contrast

**Meningioma**
- Most common extra-axial mass
- Benign, slow growing = good prognosis
- Female > Male
- Middle age
- Associated with NF-2
- Signs
	- Hyperdense
	- +/- calcifications
	- +/- edema
	- Enhances with contrast
	- Dural tail
