**Characteristics of an Acute Fracture**
- Abrupt discontinuity of all or part of cortex
	- +/- acute angulation of normally smooth contour
- Fracture lines are black and linear
	- BUT acute in angulation where fracture changes course
- Edges are jagged, rough, and/or irregular
	- NOT well-corticated

---
**Describing a Fracture**
- Direction
	- Transverse
		- Perpendicular to long axis of bone
	- Diagonal/Oblique
		- Same direction to long axis of bone
	- Longitudinal
		- Parallel to long axis of bone
	- Spiral
		- Torque injury

- Relationship of the Fracture Fragments
	- Displacement
		- Offset of distal and proximal fragments
		- Think front-to-back and side-to-side displacement
		- Described by percent or fraction of shaft width
	- Angulation
		- Angle between distal and proximal fragments
		- Described by degrees and position
			- A/P or M/L
	- Shortening
		- If any, how much overlap between fracture fragments
		- Described in cm
	- Rotation
		- Pertains to long bones
		- Describes the orientation of one joint compared to the joint on the other end
			- i.e. AP at knee and lateral at ankle

- Number of Fracture Fragments
	- Simple Fracture = 2 fragments
	- Comminuted Fracture = >2 fragments

- Communication of Fracture with the Outside
	- Closed = no communication with atmosphere
	- Open = communication with atmosphere
