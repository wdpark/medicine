**Normal Gas Pattern**
- Source
	- Swallowed air (majority)
	- Bacterial fermentation from food

| Organ       | Gas Normally?     | Air-Fluid Levels |
| ----------- | ----------------- | ---------------- |
| Stomach     | Yes               | Yes              |
| Small bowel | Yes, 1-2 loops    | Yes              |
| Large bowel | Yes, rectosigmoid | No               |

| Small Bowel                        | Large Bowel                                  |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Centrally located                  | Peripherally located                         |
| Valvulae extend across lumen       | Haustral markings do NOT extend across lumen |
| Valvulae are spaced close together | Haustral markings are widely spaced apart    |
| Normal diameter 3 cm               | Normal diameter 6 cm (9 cm cecum)            |

**Abnormal Gas Pattern**
- Localized Ileus
	- Focal irritation of loops of bowel --> adjacent inflammation
	- Causes
		- Cholecystitis
		- Pancreatitis
		- Appendicitis
		- Diverticulitis
	- Signs
		- 1-2 dilated loops of small bowel (>3 cm)
		- Air-fluid levels
		- Usually gas in rectum and/or sigmoid colon

- Adynamic Ileus
	- Entire bowel is aperistaltic or hypoperistaltic
	- Causes
		- Post-op ileus 2/2 abdominal surgery
	- Signs
		- Diffuse small and large bowel dilatation
		- Air-fluid levels
		- Usually gas in rectum and/or sigmoid colon

- Mechanical Small Bowel Obstruction
	- Causes
		- Post-op adhesions (most common)
		- Malignancy (gastric, colon, ovarian)
		- Inguinal hernia
		- Gallstone ileus
		- Ileocolic intussusception
		- Inflammatory bowel disease
	- Signs
		- Multiple dilated loops of small bowel
			- Step-ladder sign
		- Numerous air-fluid levels
		- Decompressed distal small bowel --> no gas in rectum
